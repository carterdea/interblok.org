Repurposing shipping containers to rebuild orphanages and lives

interBLOK is a nonprofit that upcycles shipping containers to provide immediate care & long term infrastructure for orphans internationally.

The Story

In May of 2014, family members of Graham and Alex came back from a relief trip to Manila, Philippines. Alex saw a great need in the way we educate and care for orphans and children internationally. During this same time Graham had been exploring the world of architecture, upcycled building materials, and renewable resources. Enter the Shipping Container.

The Model

The shipping container is stronger, safer, and faster to the field than traditional building materials. Taking modern prefabrication methods to shipping containers brings access to steel infrastructure to every port in the world.

We work with established orphanages and impoverished communities in Latin America to increase their capacity to provide for the children there.

As we work with local grassroot NGO's we bring design and supplies that are influenced by their needs. We source labor and materials locally to stimulate the economy and create jobs. International orphanage and community needs range from playgrounds to full building projects.

The Progress

We have started a new partnership with a free health clinic in La Misión. This location will service the surrounding areas as well as orphanages.

With further funding, we are preparing to start providing for an orphanage of 35 children in Tecate starting in 2016.
